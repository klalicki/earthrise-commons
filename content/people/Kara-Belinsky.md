## About Kara

Kara studies the behavior, ecology, and conservation of songbirds. Her dissertation research focused on the evolution of plumage color and singing performance in two species of wood warblers, the chestnut-sided warbler and the common yellowthroat. She has also studied the singing behavior of a thrush species, the veery. Veeries sing acoustically complex songs that feature multiple frequencies sung simultaneously. To understand how this language works, Kara records natural veery singing behavior and conducts playback experiments to test hypotheses about the function of specific songs and calls.

At New Paltz, Kara has been studying how human development of the land (urbanization) affects wildlife, with a focus on birds. She and her students hope to learn how to create a more bird-friendly campus, village, and world through this research. Their work includes studying birds at bird feeders and nest boxes (birdhouses), as well as capturing, banding, and releasing wild birds in nearby forests. To learn more, check out her lab's Instagram account @newpaltzornithology.
